1.2.1. Mathematical Induction

1.2.1 数学归纳法

Let P(n) be some statement about the integer n; for example, P(n) might be "n times (n + 3) is an even number", or "if n ≥ 10, then 2n2^{n}2​n​​ > n3n^{3}n​3​​". Suppose we want to prove that P(n) is true for all positive integers n. An important way to do this is:

a) Give a proof that P(1) is true.

b) Give a proof that "if all of P(1), P(2),..., P(n)" are true, then P(n+1) is also true"; this proof should be valid for any positive integer n.
